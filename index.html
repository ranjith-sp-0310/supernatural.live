Why We Fall for Those Who Don’t Fall for Us

We know it’s a mistake. We know it’ll hurt. Yet, we hold on. Why?

1. We Want What We Can’t Have

The harder it is to get, the more valuable it seems. Our brain tricks us into believing, "If it’s rare, it must be special."

“We don’t chase love. We chase validation wrapped in heartbreak.”

2. Love Feels Like a Drug

Science proves it—love triggers the same brain chemicals as **cocaine**. A small glance, a message, or a smile keeps you **hooked** on someone who doesn’t care.

“Rejection isn’t just emotional. It activates the same brain pathways as physical pain.”

3. The Trap of False Hope

A. The ‘What If’ Illusion

We think, "Maybe they’ll change their mind." Reality check: They won’t. Either they want you, or they don’t.

“Hoping for change doesn’t change anything.”

B. Fear of Letting Go

Even one-sided love feels better than **nothing**. Letting go means accepting it was never real.

“We hold on to pain because it’s better than emptiness.”

C. The Movie Lie

We’re taught that persistence wins love. But in real life, **love isn’t something you chase**—it’s mutual.

“Love that needs chasing isn’t love. It’s obsession.”

What Now?

Unrequited love **drains you**. At some point, you have to ask: "Am I holding on because I believe they’ll change, or because I’m scared to move on?"

Here’s the truth: If someone truly wanted you, you wouldn’t have to convince them.

“Real love isn’t earned. It’s freely given.”

Letting go isn’t easy, but staying stuck in false hope is worse. The sooner you walk away, the sooner you find **something real**.
